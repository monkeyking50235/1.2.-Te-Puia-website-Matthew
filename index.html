Visit Te Puia today!

Te Puia has many interesting places to explore, with a geothermal valley and two national schools, one of which teaches weaving, and the other teaches carving.

Two prominant places found in Te puia are the weaving and carving schools, which are national schools of Aotearoa that teach treasured skills to be used for generations. Another location that can be found in Te Puia is their geo-thermal valley housing numerous geysers and mud-pools.

At the National Weaving school, students are taught the skills and traditions of a craft hundreds of years old. As well as learning how to weave harakeke (flax) and other materials, students learn the stories and designs unique to each iwi (tribe), as well as the MƒÅori protocols associated with weaving. These include planting according to the phases of the moon and reciting prayers of thanks for flax and trees used. Conversly, the National Carving school tutors carving students for three years, under the guidance of those, such as Clive Fugill and James Rickard, master carvers at Te Puia who were once institute carving students themselves.
Te Puia's geo-thermal valley is filled with lots to explore, which makes it a great place to bring your family. With over 500 pools and 65 geyser vents, you will never have a dull moment.
